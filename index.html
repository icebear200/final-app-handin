Summary paragraph outlining what was most/least successful

Revolve allowed users to sort and filter on almost every attribute which allowed users to come up with specified options. Expedia and Shein both included a wide range of sorting and filtering options and all three sites included a choice to aggregate with a wishlist and or shopping cart. The most helpful was allowing users to sort by popularity/ratings as well as price. The most helpful filtering was allowing users to filter by category such as dress/top for Revovle and Shein or apartment/hotel for Expedia. All sites had multiple sorting/filtering criteria which made sorting and filtering not mutually exclusive, an attirbute I'd like to implement. Additionally, all sites allowed users to filter by multiple attributes. Though this helps display options that match the filtering criteria, it seems a bit redundant and takes away from the point of filtering if the filter options display products that satisfy multiple criteria because it makes the outcome less specific. I liked how Shein and Revolve allowed users to clear/reset individual filters and also had the option to clear all filters.
